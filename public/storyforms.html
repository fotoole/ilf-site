LIBERIA FLOWERS

Housing Opportunities for Women

“I’m an ex-felon—two-time ex-felon—and on disability. I’ve been on disability since I was 17 and a half...I’ve seen how it be, how it is to be rock bottom, sleep on benches, hang out in hallways, be around the wrong folks and people, and be in and out of the jail."

“...Right now, the program I’m in is Shelter Care Plus Permanent Housing... I’m going to Truman College for GED classes...I come to any class that will give me a certificate and that I feel like I accomplished something, because the majority of my life growing up, I come from a dysfunctional family to where, I always wanted to accomplish things [but] I never got a chance to achieve and here [at HOW] I’ve been achieving my goals."

ANDREA HALL

Staff member, Chicago Coalition for the Homeless

“A goal in my life is to let people know there are different faces of homelessness, and when they first meet me, people won’t think...that I was homeless.”

CHARLES JENKINS

Volunteer, Chicago Coalition for the Homeless

“About 1990, I got involved in Chicago Coalition for the Homeless. It inspired me. It woke up something socially conscious and just in me. It inspired me to get my life in order. So I went into detox and got myself clean and joined the 12 step program. I’ve been an avid member since that time to this very day.

“I’m not looking to do anything spectacular. Most of my life is behind [me] anyway, you know...Society will ultimately, hopefully, be able to encourage some of our public officials to be a bit people-concerned for a change. I don’t mean lobbyists or big industry you know, but grassroots, everyday people.”

VALERIE GALVAN

Housing Opportunities for Women

“I’m working harder now to get my other children back home with me. Rebuilding my relationships with them and my family . . . from a life of darkness to a life of light. You have to stay focused, keep it positive all times, and don’t give up. That’s the main thing.

“I can’t express myself to how grateful I am to be where I am at today, with all the opportunities that were given to me. I could have gotten worse. I could be dead or anything. Coming from the streets, I am doing real good compared to where I have been.”
