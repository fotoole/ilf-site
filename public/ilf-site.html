Imagine Your Future

The Ignatian Legacy Leaders Fellows Program is an invitation to imagine your future. Look back on your career in leadership and mine from those experiences to determine your next step, all while deepening your faith journey. Who can benefit from the wisdom and skills that you have acquired?

Now is your time to examine the world’s problems and address them–through the eyes of Jesuit network leaders who are grappling with those problems. Together we’ll discover Jesuit responses to poverty, injustice, the impacts of technology, the need for entrepreneurship, and sustainability. You will be exposed to “best thinking” while deciding where you want to invest your legacy leadership.

Program Overview

The Ignatian Legacy Fellows Program is a yearlong program that includes personal reflection, guided meditation, spiritual readings and discussion, and volunteer service – all pointed towards helping participants better understand where they might be called to use their gifts in service of others.

Four-day residency sessions are held every two months. These highly dialogical and interactive sessions encourage participants to discuss theological texts, engage with thought-provoking speakers, and share life experiences. Journaling, online reading, and response work complement the sessions.

In addition to these residencies, participants will also embark on a service immersion trip to a Jesuit mission in Lima, Peru. The program culminates with a pilgrimage to Spain, to visit Barcelona, St. Ignatius’ birthplace, and Manresa, where he wrote the Spiritual Exercises.

Rooted in the
Spiritual Exercises

Using the Spiritual Exercises of Saint Ignatius Loyola as a model, this yearlong program offers an immersive experience. The Spiritual Exercises are the cornerstone of Ignatian Spirituality, and focus on a way of understanding and living one’s relationship with God in the world. St. Ignatius — founder of the Society of Jesus, the Jesuit order of priests — encouraged his community to go out to the margins to push the boundaries of understanding and engagement.

For 450 years, the Jesuits have built their brand on discerning and deciding ways to proceed at various stages of life by recognizing one’s gifts and the world’s needs, and how these two things intersect. Similarly, the Ignatian Legacy Fellowship Program invites participants to “collect themselves” and “move out” in mission to engage and transform themselves and the world.

Your Journey

The program’s three-part exploration moves leaders in a 20-person cohort from worldly concerns of commerce and competition toward the burgeoning spiritual concerns of retirement in the third chapter of life: service to family, community, posterity, and God.

The Inward Journey

The journey begins with personal reflection and meditations that help participants better understand themselves and where their lives are calling them. Through guided spiritual direction, the first sessions borrow from the Exercises’ Jesuit meditation practice. Participants deepen their respect for their interior lives through contemplation and prayer to become, in St. Ignatius’s words, “contemplatives in action.”

Communing with Others

The journey continues through group interactions that help participants discover new ways of understanding and directing their life experiences to support causes and programs beyond their current spheres. They share their stories and contemplations with their spiritual directors and other participants by discussing readings and reflections.

Turning Outward

In the final stage of this journey, participants turn outward toward the broader community as they better understand the underlying theology that supports the mission of faith-based institutions such as hospitals, universities, schools, and social programs. Each segment of our travels will introduce participants to institutional and mission leaders, with opportunities for dialogue about their current organizational challenges that will invite engagement and consultation from the wealth of experience and wisdom of the participants. Through these interactions, legacy fellows learn about the threats and opportunities confronting these mission-driven institutions, in order to apply their own expertise in management, marketing, organizational development, fundraising, and strategic planning to meet these challenges.
