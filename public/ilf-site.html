Do you want to create an intentional and meaningful retirement?

Will you go it alone or would you like to accompany a cohort of Ignatian Legacy Fellows as they engage in a transition process to the third chapter of their lives?

The Ignatian Legacy Leaders Program is a yearlong program that includes personal reflection, guided meditation, spiritual readings and discussion, and volunteer service – all pointed towards helping participants better understand where they might be called to use their gifts in service of others.

About

Participants

C-suite executives, organizational leaders, and nonprofit executives nearing or in retirement, who want to use their accumulated skills and expertise in service of others, regardless of their faith tradition

Schedule

Four-day retreat sessions are held every two months. A full schedule of the six retreat sessions will be sent to those who apply. Four weekends are within the USA. Two additional weeks involve travel to Lima, Peru, to visit the work of the Jesuits in that country and the final experience for the Fellows will be a pilgrimage to Spain and Rome.

Approach

The Ignatian Legacy Fellows program takes its shape from the plan of the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius—preparing oneself spiritually, intellectually, and professionally.

Spiritual Guidance

The Exercises are designed to assist the individual facing an important life-decision. They begin with and build upon a careful examination of one’s life experiences and interior life. They encourage a careful examination of one’s motives and values. They foster the interior freedom that allows one to move forward with gratitude for one’s past and a deepening of one’s commitments toward the future. Expert guides are provided for the newcomer to the Exercises.

Intellectual Stimulation

The highly dialogical and interactive sessions encourage participants to engage assigned readings and the presentations of thought-provoking speakers, as well as discussions with faculty and students.

Sharing Professional Expertise

Each of the weekend sessions is composed of visits to programs dealing with the significant issues of the day, and especially with the training of young leaders. How are the Jesuits attempting to prepare men and women dedicated to the common good as opposed to a narrow focus on personal benefit? What are the obstacles to accomplishing this and maintaining programs that foster a new kind of civic leader?

The Journey

Moving away from the world of daily responsibilities of management and leadership, Ignatian Legacy Fellows come together six times over the course of a calendar year to explore the meaning of retirement and the opportunities that lie ahead.

The Journey involves a turn inward.

It begins with personal reflection and a chance to explore with others on a similar journey, and with experts in spiritual direction, how God has acted in one’s life, and how God may be calling us toward a new chapter that involves sharing one’s time and talent with those in need. Using the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius, Fellows will have the opportunity to deepen their own spiritual lives at this critical juncture in their own journey.

The Journey involves moving with a cohort of fellow leaders.

Ignatian Legacy Fellows will be not only engaging with one another, but will be learning the theological and spiritual roots that underlie the faith-based works of Jesuits in education, health care, and social welfare, as well as the international dimensions of the work of the Society of Jesus.

The Journey involves turning toward the broader community.

Each of the six sessions is designed to introduce the cohort to the issues and concerns faced by the leadership of these institutions and programs. Together, the Fellows and the local leaders of faith-based programs will share insights, opportunities, and strategies for handling today’s leadership challenges based on their own experience in management, marketing, organizational development, fundraising, and strategic planning.

At the conclusion of the year-long program, Ignatian Legacy Fellows will be invited to become a member of the Society of Ignatian Fellows, a forum for the exchange of ideas in the spirituality and mission of the Society of Jesus and its works.
