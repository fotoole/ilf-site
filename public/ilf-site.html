Do you want to create an intentional and meaningful retirement?

Will you go it alone or would you like to accompany a cohort of Ignatian Legacy Fellows who are engaged in the transition to the third chapter of life?

The Ignatian Legacy Fellows Program is a yearlong program hosted by Loyola University Chicago, Santa Clara University, and Georgetown University that includes personal reflection, guided meditation, spiritual readings and discussion, and volunteer service – that all point toward helping participants better understand where they might be called to use their gifts in service to others.

About

Approach

The Ignatian Legacy Fellows program takes its shape from the plan of the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius—preparing oneself spiritually, intellectually, and professionally.

Spiritual Guidance

The Spiritual Exercises are designed to assist the individual in better understanding the purpose of his or her life. They begin with and build upon a careful examination of one’s life experiences and interior life and encourage a careful examination of one’s motives and values. They foster the interior freedom that allows one to move forward with gratitude for one’s past while deepening one’s commitments toward the future. Expert guides are provided for the newcomer to the Exercises.

Intellectual Stimulation

The highly dialogical and interactive sessions encourage participants to engage with assigned readings and the presentations by thought-provoking speakers, as well as discussions with faculty and students.

Sharing Professional Expertise

Each of the weekend sessions is composed of visits to programs dealing with the significant issues of the day, especially with the training of young leaders. How are the Jesuits attempting to prepare men and women dedicated to the common good as opposed to a narrow focus on personal benefit? What are the obstacles to accomplishing this and maintaining programs that foster a new kind of civic leader?

Participants

Our participants are C-suite executives, organizational leaders, and nonprofit executives nearing or in retirement, who want to use their accumulated skills and expertise in service to others, regardless of their faith tradition.

Schedule

The full schedule of the six retreat sessions will be sent to those who apply. The schedule includes four four-day retreat sessions every two months in the United States and two weeklong sessions: one involves travel to Lima, Peru, to visit the work of the Jesuits in that country, and the final weeklong session is a pilgrimage to Spain and Rome.

The Journey

Moving away from the world of daily responsibilities of management and leadership, Ignatian Legacy Fellows come together six times over the course of a calendar year to explore the meaning of retirement and the opportunities that lie ahead.

The Journey involves a turn inward

It begins with personal reflection and a chance to explore with others on a similar journey how God has acted in one’s life and how God may be calling us toward a new chapter that involves sharing one’s time and talent with those in need. Using the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius, Fellows, with the guidance of experts in spiritual direction, will have the opportunity to deepen their own spiritual lives at this critical juncture in their own journey.

The Journey involves moving with a cohort of fellow leaders

Ignatian Legacy Fellows will be not only engaging with one another, but will be learning the theological and spiritual roots that underlie the faith-based works of Jesuits in education, health care, and social welfare, as well as the international dimensions of the work of the Society of Jesus.

The Journey involves turning toward the broader community

Together, the Fellows and the local leaders of faith-based programs will share insights, opportunities, and strategies for handling today’s leadership challenges based on their own experience in management, marketing, organizational development, fundraising, and strategic planning. Each of the six sessions is designed to introduce the cohort to the issues and concerns faced by the leadership of these institutions and programs.

At the conclusion of the yearlong program, Ignatian Legacy Fellows will be invited to become members of the Society of Ignatian Fellows, an ongoing forum for the exchange of ideas in the spirituality and mission of the Society of Jesus and its works.
